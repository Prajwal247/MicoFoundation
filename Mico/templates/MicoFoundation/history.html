Our History

Holding the enviable record of being the oldest teacher training institution in the Western Hemisphere, The Mico University College stands as a beacon of excellence in delivering quality teacher education in Jamaica, the wider Caribbean region and beyond, for over nineteen decades. The Institution is therefore, an embodiment of resilience, endurance and relevance especially in the 21st century. This solid foundation is enshrined in the College’s Strategic Plan, which envisions The Mico achieving full University status and offering degrees at all levels in science, technology, the liberal arts, and the humanities.

A significant aspect of the stability of The Mico since its inception in 1836 is the constant support of the Lady Mico Trust. The Trust ensures consistency in the oversight of the ownership and the management of the College. During the 1970s, the College began a programme of development and expansion. This resulted in the need for the acquisition of more properties and other tangible assets. The then Principal, Dr. Errol Lawrence Miller, encouraged the Board of Directors to establish a locally managed entity in which assets, which were not acquired through government subventions, could be privately managed. A foundation was considered a suitable solution to this idea.

In 1981 The Mico Foundation was established as a registered company under the Companies’ Act of Jamaica, with registered offices at 1A Marescaux Road. The objectives were to provide a safe and efficient system to own, manage and protect the newly acquired assets, as well as to protect the legacy of The Lady Mico Trust. Additionally, the Foundation would raise funds to assist with the capital development and maintenance of the Mico and the Foundation.

The Foundation started with thirty members, drawn in equal numbers from The Mico Past Students’ Association, The Mico University College and the Lady Mico Trust. A nine-member Board of Directors with an equal numbers of representative from the. foregoing bodies governed the Foundation. Its first Chairman was Mr. Lemuel Augustus Lindo, Principal Errol Miller was the first Managing Director and Cynthia Graham was the first Executive Secretary. A member of the Trustees, the Accountant of the Foundation and the Executive Secretary sit on the Board of Directors as non-voting members.

The successes of the Foundation between 1981 and 1999 attracted the attention and interest of the Lady Mico Trustees who were impressed with its work and management. On this premise, they give custody of the Trust property in Jamaica to the Foundation. In October 2000, the property at 1A Marescaux Road on which The Mico University College is also located, was transferred from the Lady Mico Trust in England to The Mico Foundation. The Foundation now stands as a formidable entity and chief stakeholder in the ownership and management of the properties of The Mico. It focuses primarily on the development, expansion and management of its tangible and intangible assets and the preservation of the heritage of the Lady Mico Trust.

In 2014, the Board of Directors of the Foundation reviewed the governance and operations of the Foundation and made significant changes to the articles of incorporation to ensure more independently managed operations. As a result, the Principal of the college is no longer the Managing Director of the Foundation, and a Secretary/Manager was appointed to manage the affairs of the Foundation. The First Secretary Manager under the new governance structure was Mr. Laban Roomes. Careful consideration was also given to the selection of the Board of Directors to ensure a neutral relationship between the Board of the University College and the Board of the Foundation.

The Board of the Foundation operates with three standing committees:: Financial Management, Projects Management and Governance Committee. These committees oversee the policies of the Foundation to ensure transparency and integrity in its leadership. The Secretary/ Manager of the Foundation heads a small staff that sees to the daily conduct of business.

The Mico Foundation earns income through its commercial enterprises, projects implementations and management, and the rental of its premises. The funds raised are used for managing the affairs of the Foundation, improving the University College’s campus, granting of scholarships and other financial assistance to students and staff of The Mico University College.

Vision Statement

The Vision of the Mico Foundation is:

To be an active, dependable and viable source for endowment, economic enterprises, assets preservation and an enabling environment for the development of social capital

Mission Statement

It is the Mission of the Mico Foundation to:

Develop, expand and manage the tangible and intangible assets of the Foundation and preserve the heritage of the Lady Mico Trust.

Create and encourage opportunities for entrepreneurship and increased financial support for the University College

Maintain an efficient system of estates and infrastructure management that will ensure optimal use by The Mico University College.

Provide avenues of support for the wellbeing and interest of Students & employees.
