Message

Secretary Manager

Mr. Burchell Duhaney J.P.

I am indeed happy to be leading the management team of The Mico Foundation at this time when The Mico University College is on the verge of changing its status to full university. As Secretary/Manager, I lead a small team of eight employees who understand the developmental roles and obligations of the Foundation and are committed to ensuring its success. In like manner, the Board of Directors has guided the directions of the Foundation through the altruistic commitment of its founders.

The development of the Mico Foundation in 1981 added a new dimension to The Mico as a trust institution. It brought into focus a perspective on how the institution can acquire, property, protect its assets, and support the ongoing developmental needs. Since its inception, the Foundation has invested in the acquisition of property, the development of students accommodations, improve the technical support systems, improve the aesthetic appearance of the University College by building a new entry gate, improve the perimeter fence and grounds, improve the water supply by drilling a well, among other capital development projects. It also provides financial resources for grants and scholarships to empowering students and staff in need. A significant part of its obligation is the protection and maintenance of the legacy of the Lady Mico Trust. As a Charitable Organization, the Foundation earns its income through the support and generosity of graduates and well-wishers, project management, rental of property and other entrepreneurial enterprises. The financial gains from these enterprises go into the improvement of the infrastructure needs of the University College and the support of students, employees, and other outreach projects. It is undeniable that Mico has been outstanding in the development of human capacity in education. For this reason, it has survived over 180 years. However, the fourth industrial revolution with the onset of new technology and artificial intelligence will

bring new challenges that will call for greater resources for surviving in this new dispensation. Your financial support will be greatly needed as we move forward, particularly as we change from University College to full University. I applaud the efforts of all our stakeholders who work tirelessly in supporting the University College. The Lady Mico Trust has been consistent and steadfast in its support for more than 180 years. The Mico Foundation as a Charitable non-profit organization champions the cause for infrastructure development, and property and assets management, The vibrant presence of The Mico Alumni Association Limited (MOSA) contributing to the protection of the legacy, and The Mico University College Foundation of the America (MUCFA), established as an IRS approved 501 (c) (3) non-profit organizations in the United State of America, giving opportunities for making your tax-deductible donations of funds, gifts, goods and services to The Mico.
